The Yeoman of the Guard

ACT II [SCENE.— The same— Moonlight.] [Two days have elapsed.]
CHORUS

Night has spread her pall once more, And the pris'ner still is free: Open is his dungeon door, Useless now his dungeon key. He has shaken off his yoke— How, no mortal man can tell! Shame on loutish jailor-folk

DAME

Warders are ye? Whom do ye ward? Warders are ye? Whom do ye ward? Bolt, bar, and key, Shackle and cord, Fetter and chain, Dungeon and stone, All are in vain— Prisoner's flown! Spite of ye all, he is free— he is free! Whom do ye ward? Pretty warders are ye!

WOMEN

Night has spread her pall once more, And the pris'ner still is free:

WILFRED

Aye, it's well for thee to laugh. Thou hast a good post, and hast cause to be merry.

POINT

She did nothing of the kind, so hold thy peace and perpend. Now, while he liveth she is dead to me and I to her, and so, my jibes and jokes notwithstanding, I am the saddest and the sorriest dog in England!

FAIRFAX

Well, Sergeant Meryll, and how fares thy pretty charge,Elsie Maynard?

DAME

Nay, Sergeant Meryll, don't go. I have something of grave import to say to thee.

ALL

He is free! He is free! Pretty warders are ye/we!
